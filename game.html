// about fictive //
fictive is a reimagining of the classic beloved Harry Potter story. Harry Potter, the eponymous character of the series, has always been unambiguously assumed to be straight. However, in many fanfiction adaptations of the novels, Harry is recast as queer, gender non-conforming, or sometimes even a POC. In fact, a major strength of fanfiction lies in its ability to create media representation and normalize underrepresented identities and experiences. We added a higher level of agency by turning traditional fanfiction into this text-based choose-your-own-adventure game -- now, you can actively control how the story ends based on how you want to respond, conversing with Harry to find out what exactly made him so nervous talk to you. Depending on what you say, his comfort level increments or decrements, and his responses could become more terse or relaxed. We carefully modeled our Harry after the novel’s Harry Potter, a nuanced character whose awkwardness and impetuousness is well-known, only changing one thing -- his sexuality.

// about your score //
The game tracks Harry's comfort level depending on how you respond to him—each response affects how he feels around you, much like a person would think and be affected by their surroundings.

Harry's max comfort level is 16—even if you were able to get Harry to tell you his secret, if you didn't get 11, some of your responses held biases or aroused feelings of discomfort. See if you can play again and recognize these responses and raise Harry's comfort level.
